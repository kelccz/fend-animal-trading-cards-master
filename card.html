Cat (Domestic cat)

Because cats were venerated in ancient Egypt, they were commonly believed to have been domesticated there, but there may have been instances of domestication as early as the Neolithic from around 9,500 years ago.

  • Scientific Name: Felis catus
  • Avarage Lenght: 46 centimeters
  • Avarage Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: They can live on the highest mountains and in the hottest deserts

The domestic cat is a member of the cat family, the felids.

Cats have either a mutualistic or commensal relationship with humans. Two main theories are given about how cats were domesticated. In one, people deliberately tamed cats in a process of artificial selection as they were useful predators of vermin. The alternative idea is that cats were simply tolerated by people and gradually diverged from their wild relatives through natural selection, as they adapted to hunting the vermin found around humans in towns and villages.
